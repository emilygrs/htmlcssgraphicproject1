Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS)

A condition that's very common and very individual to each woman that has it

PCOS stands for Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome. It is one of the most common hormonal conditions in women, according to The Hormone Health Network, part of the Endrocrine Society, which is located in Washington, D.C.

In an August 2019 article by Good Morning America (GMA), Dr. Jennifer Ashton, an OB/GYN, discussed polycystic ovarian syndrome. "Estimates are anywhere from 5% to upwards of 15% of women," she said, in reference to the amount of women affected by this disorder. Regardless of age, PCOS can affect a female that is in her teenage years, as well as someone who is reaching the end of her reproductive years, menopause. "I think in general it can affect three organ systems," Ashton said, in regards to what PCOS affects in a woman's body. "PCOS can affect your period," she explained. "They can be too close together. They can be too far apart. And just for kicks, they can be normal and you can still have PCOS. But I want to emphasize that today, with reproductive technologies, seeing a reproductive endocrinologist, or an REI specialist - women with PCOS can get pregnant."

The second organ system that PCOS can affect is the metabolic system in a woman's body. "For reasons that are very complicated and not well understood yet, we know that women with PCOS have a five to seven-time increase risk of developing type-2 diabetes later in life," Ashton mentioned. "Good nutrition, fitness are very important. Keeping your weight in a healthy range is really critical if you have PCOS. It can be more difficult for women with PCOS to metabolize carbohydrates, so typically being on a low-carb diet is beneficial for women with PCOS."

The last and third area of the body that is affected is what Ashton deems as "other." "These are usually affects caused by a high testosterone level. So that means acne and excessive body hair. The good news there are tons of treatments for hormonal acne. Topicals, orals, birth control pills, as a class all lower testosterone levels. Over time, they all improve acne. The body hair can be subtle and tricky to diagnose. "In terms of treatment there are so many options," she said. "Creams, lasers, birth control pills, over time will also slow that hair growth."

In order to diagnose a young girl or woman with PCOS, Ashton adds that the diagnosis is based on a number of factors. "Physical exam, the history of the patient. What she'll tell us about her periods, her weight, her skin, and things like that. Blood tests are a very easy way to help complete the picture." Also the one thing that Ashton notes is crucial is that a person who would like to be tested for PCOS, can't be on birth control. "That kind of throws off the whole picture. PCOS is also not a routine testing. It's only if a woman is having a particular sign symptom or problem, that we then go looking for it." "

Common Symptoms of PCOS

Disclaimer: Not all women experience the same symptoms of PCOS. This is just a guideline of many symptoms that a woman can experience. Not all of the symptoms for PCOS are listed here.

Acne is a common symptom for women with PCOS, as the body can become hormone imbalanced, which can lead to acne and skin sensitivities.

Feeling Tired is something that many women with PCOS come across as the body can experience a form of insulin resistance, due to the hormone imbalance, which causes the the body to feel fatigued and tired.

Infertility is a symptom of PCOS, but not an issue for every woman that has PCOS. Some experience infertility, other's do not.

Hair Growth is another symptom that women can experience due to the raised levels of androgen (testosterone) that builds up in the bloodstream. Also called "hirsutism", hair growth in unwanted places, such as the chin and the neck, due to increased testosterone levels, are caused by the hormone imbalance that happens with PCOS.

Weight Gain is one of the more typical symptoms of PCOS, as when the body is experiencing a combination of insulin resistance and hormone imbalance, the body's response is stress, which can lead to weight gain.

Irregular Menstrual Cycles are a symptom of PCOS that involve a change in the pattern of a woman's menstral cycle. Some women with PCOS can have missed periods also. Like infertility, they are not a symptom for every woman that has PCOS.

Treatments for PCOS

Disclaimer: Many woman with PCOS respond differently to treatments. It is always best to consult an OB/GYN or other experts, before doing any sort of medical treatment listed below.

Acupuncture is a form of traditional Chinese medicine that is used to treat PCOS and other disorders. Acupuncture involves the gentle touch of needles to the skin, that help to decrease symptoms and lower the sympathetic nervous system activity.

Laser Hair Removal is very common amongst women with PCOS, as unwanted hair can show up in a variety of places, even with other methods like birth control being used. Laser hair removal, or what doctors call 'electrolysis' is a method to destroy the hair follicle. The process is done over a period of multiple sessions.

Birth Control is not only used as a form of contraception. Birth control contains high amounts of estrogen and progesterin, which are hormones that are similar to the one's made by the ovaries. As those with PCOS have higher levels of testosterone, the addition of estrogen and progesterin help to balance overall levels in the body.

Eating Well For You is an important step in helping to decrease PCOS symptoms. Whatever form of healthy eating that helps lower your symptoms is the best for you, do it. Everyone is different, and no two healthy eating plans are the same. However, there are guidelines out there as to what is best to eat when you have PCOS.

Fitness/Exercise is a crucial step for balancing one's PCOS symptoms. Being that a symptom of PCOS is weight gain, it's important to get a varied amount of exercise throughout the day. When you have PCOS, exercise should be your friend, and not your enemy. It is recommended that those with PCOS, should have at least 30 minutes of exercise a day.

Me Time is one of the most important parts to healing the body from PCOS. Give yourself some much-needed time to relax and enjoy. Whether that's going outdoors, walking the dog, doing a fun activity, its good for the soul and adds more balance to your life. It's important to figure out how to manage your symptoms and be healthy and happy.
